Virtual Private Networks

A virtual private network is an encrypted private connection between two machines. When you use a VPN, your device connects to a server. This will allow your device to use that server's information to browse the internet. This means that any site trying to track your information will see the VPN's server information instead of yours.

Advantages of VPNs

Privacy - VPNs conceal your IP address, they hide your network information and are a reliable way to protect yourself from malicious hackers

Security - Employees who work remotely can use a VPN to have a secure connection to their company's applications and files

Disadvantages of VPNs

Decreases internet speed - Slow connection speed can be frustrating, especially for gamers

Blocked Access - Certain content providers use VPN blocking software to prevent VPN users from accessing their content

Data leaks - Information leaks can occur if the VPN is not setup correctly

VPN prices vary but they can be expensive
