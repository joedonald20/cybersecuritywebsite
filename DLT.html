Distributed Ledger Technology

Distributed Ledger Technology is a data storage mechanism in which data is stored at multiple locations on a shared network. It provides a transparent and secure method of exchnaging money. It uses cryptography to securely store information. A decentralised ledger is immune to a cyber-attack, as all the copies across the network are spread globally, and they need to be attacked simultaneously for the attack to be successful.

Advantages of DLT

Security - Distributed ledgers are safe, as they are decentralised and use high tech security. Nodes that enter malicious data will get expelled from the network

Transparency - All participants can view every transaction

Real life application - DLT can help governments with tax collection and the issuance of passports

Disadvantages of DLT

High energy consumption - DLT requires a high amount of computer power to complete signature verification

Scalability issues - High transaction costs and low speeds are hard to work with on a major scale

Cost - It can be expensive to complete a transaction
